# TCM Inquiry Guidance

## Core Intake Fields
Structured inquiry should capture chief complaint, duration, onset pattern, triggers, relieving factors, symptom changes, and whether the user has already stated clear negatives. These fields are factual intake notes and must not be overwritten by retrieved evidence.

## Digestive Discomfort Inquiry
For stomach discomfort, bloating, reflux-like discomfort, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, constipation, or abdominal pain, ask about duration, location, severity trend, appetite, stool pattern, urination, sleep, fever, bleeding, and whether symptoms are worsening.

## Sleep Appetite Stool Urine
Sleep, appetite, stool, and urination are supporting inquiry fields. They help complete the intake summary but do not by themselves create a diagnosis, prescription, or treatment promise.

## Follow-up Question Selection
When key fields are missing, ask one focused follow-up question. Do not repeatedly ask for fields already answered by the user. Preserve user-stated negations such as no fever, no chest pain, or no blood in stool.
